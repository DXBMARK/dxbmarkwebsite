This Acceptable Use Policy defines the types of use that are prohibited on DXBMARK services and platforms. Violation of this policy may result in suspension or termination of service, account cancellation, legal action, and liability for damages.

## 3. General Prohibited Uses

You must not use DXBMARK services for:

### 3.1 Illegal Activity

- Any use that violates applicable laws, regulations, or court orders
- Activities that constitute a crime in any jurisdiction
- Violation of local, state, federal, or international law
- Money laundering, fraud, or financing of illegal activities
- Facilitating or enabling illegal activities

### 3.2 Fraud and Deception

- Fraud, identity theft, or impersonation
- Misrepresentation of your identity or authorization
- Deceptive practices or dishonest dealings
- Creating fake accounts or misleading profiles
- Phishing, social engineering, or manipulation

### 3.3 Harassment and Abuse

- Threats, harassment, bullying, or abusive behavior
- Hate speech or discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Sexual harassment or unwelcome sexual content
- Stalking or doxxing (publishing private information without consent)
- Coordinated harassment campaigns

### 3.4 Violence and Harm

- Content depicting or promoting violence, self-harm, or suicide
- Threats of violence or harm against any person
- Glorification or encouragement of violence
- Content that could reasonably cause severe emotional distress

## 4. Technical Abuse and Attacks

You must not:

### 4.1 Malware and Viruses

- Create, distribute, or upload malware, viruses, worms, trojans, or other malicious code
- Compromise, infect, or damage systems or networks
- Execute denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks or network flooding
- Conduct unauthorized penetration testing or vulnerability exploitation
- Create or distribute phishing emails or phishing pages

### 4.2 System Abuse

- Attempt to gain unauthorized access to DXBMARK systems or customer systems
- Circumvent security measures, firewalls, or access controls
- Scan, probe, or enumerate systems without authorization
- Reverse-engineer, decompile, or extract source code of DXBMARK services
- Monitor network traffic or conduct packet sniffing without authorization
- Disable security features, remove restrictions, or bypass authentication

### 4.3 API and Automation Abuse

- Use APIs or automations in a way that exceeds provider rate limits or technical quotas
- Violate third-party platform terms through DXBMARK integrations or automations
- Use automated tools that degrade DXBMARK service availability or performance for other users
- Circumvent access controls, authentication, or usage restrictions through API calls
- Use integrations or automation workflows to extract, replicate, or mirror DXBMARK service functionality in an unauthorized way

### 4.4 Resource Abuse

- Consume excessive bandwidth, server resources, or system capacity beyond authorized limits
- Use DXBMARK services for cryptocurrency mining, rendering, or computationally intensive tasks not authorized by service terms
- Deploy scraping bots or automated tools that harm service performance
- Create thousands of accounts or spam registrations
- Use DXBMARK services as a proxy or VPN without authorization
